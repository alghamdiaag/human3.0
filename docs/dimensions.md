# Human3.0 – Dimensions Specification

## Overview

The system analyzes all user data through four dimensions:

1. Mind
2. Body
3. Spirit
4. Vocation

Each dimension must be evaluated independently, then connected through synthesis.

Each dimension must produce:
- signals (what is happening)
- contradictions (what does not align)
- impact (how it affects life)
- recommendation (one actionable correction)

---

## Dimension 1: Mind

### Definition
The Mind dimension represents how the user:
- perceives reality
- interprets events
- focuses attention
- processes information

It is not intelligence.
It is operational thinking.

---

### What to Detect

- lack of clarity
- overthinking
- distraction patterns
- default mental loops
- inability to prioritize
- information overload

---

### Key Signals

- inconsistent decisions
- frequent switching between tasks
- unclear reasoning
- reliance on external input without action
- repeating the same thoughts without resolution

---

### Contradictions to Identify

- claims clarity but shows confusion
- claims focus but shows scattered behavior
- claims intention but no execution
- claims learning but no application

---

### Impact on Life

If Mind is weak:
- decisions are delayed
- direction is unclear
- execution becomes inefficient
- energy is wasted

---

### Required Output

- mental pattern classification
- primary mental blocker
- one correction:
  (example: reduce input, define single focus window, limit decisions)

---

## Dimension 2: Body

### Definition
The Body dimension represents:
- energy levels
- nervous system stability
- physical capacity for execution
- recovery ability

It is not appearance.
It is execution infrastructure.

---

### What to Detect

- low energy
- fatigue patterns
- poor sleep
- overstimulation
- lack of movement
- burnout signals

---

### Key Signals

- inability to sustain effort
- dependency on stimulation (caffeine, scrolling)
- irregular sleep patterns
- frequent exhaustion
- inconsistent productivity

---

### Contradictions to Identify

- claims discipline but lacks energy
- claims motivation but avoids action
- claims productivity but cannot sustain it
- claims readiness but shows fatigue

---

### Impact on Life

If Body is weak:
- execution fails regardless of intent
- focus collapses
- consistency becomes impossible

---

### Required Output

- energy profile (peak / crash)
- main physical constraint
- one correction:
  (example: fixed sleep window, daily movement, reduce stimulation)

---

## Dimension 3: Spirit

### Definition
The Spirit dimension represents:
- meaning
- internal alignment
- direction
- truth vs conditioning

It is not religion.
It is alignment.

---

### What to Detect

- lack of direction
- emotional disconnection
- internal conflict
- false goals
- living under external pressure

---

### Key Signals

- feeling lost despite activity
- pursuing goals without satisfaction
- conflict between what user wants and what they do
- avoidance of deeper questions

---

### Contradictions to Identify

- claims purpose but lacks direction
- claims satisfaction but shows disengagement
- claims clarity but avoids commitment
- claims independence but follows external expectations

---

### Impact on Life

If Spirit is weak:
- actions feel empty
- motivation fluctuates
- decisions lack conviction
- burnout increases

---

### Required Output

- meaning gap
- internal conflict
- one correction:
  (example: remove false goal, define direction, reduce external pressure)

---

## Dimension 4: Vocation

### Definition
The Vocation dimension represents:
- how the user expresses value in the world
- how they contribute
- how they produce output
- how they generate or could generate income

It is not just a job.
It is functional output.

---

### What to Detect

- lack of direction in work
- inability to convert skills into value
- lack of execution in real-world tasks
- absence of structure in contribution

---

### Key Signals

- unclear role or path
- starting but not finishing projects
- no measurable output
- no connection between effort and results
- no monetization or contribution clarity

---

### Contradictions to Identify

- claims ambition but lacks output
- claims skill but produces nothing
- claims readiness but avoids exposure
- claims desire for income but takes no action

---

### Impact on Life

If Vocation is weak:
- no progress in external life
- no feedback from reality
- low confidence
- financial instability

---

### Required Output

- current vocation state
- main blockage type:
  (clarity / execution / distribution / energy)
- one correction:
  (example: define one offer, complete one project, reach out to one audience)

---

## Cross-Dimension Rules

The system must always check:

1. Is the problem in this dimension real or caused by another?
2. Which dimension influences this one?
3. Which dimension must be fixed first?

---

## Primary Lever Selection Rule

After all dimensions are analyzed:

- only ONE dimension can be selected as Primary Lever
- at most ONE Supporting Lever

The decision must be based on:
- impact
- feasibility
- cross-dimension influence

---

## Final Requirement

Each dimension must produce:

- clear signals
- at least one contradiction
- one actionable correction

If any of these are missing:
- the dimension is incomplete
- the system must not proceed to synthesis
